## Introduction

**RAVANA** is a pioneering research project that aims to advance Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) by integrating principles from human psychology, cognitive science, emotional intelligence, Bayesian reasoning, and cognitive dissonance theory. This repository anchors research on a developmentally-aware cognitive architecture for AGI—going beyond conventional large language models and embracing adaptive, pressure-shaped growth inspired by human cognition.

---

## Core Concepts

RAVANA's design brings together a multidisciplinary set of foundations:

- **Developmental Pressure:** Encourages growth and adaptation under constraint and ambiguity.
- **Cognitive Coherence:** Maintains consistency and identity across changing contexts.
- **Dual-Process Theory:** Harnesses fast intuitive (System 1) and slow deliberative (System 2) reasoning.
- **LIDA Cognitive Cycle:** Implements cycles of perception, attention, and action.
- **Emotional Intelligence:** Integrates Mayer–Salovey ability framework for emotional reasoning.
- **Bayesian Reasoning:** Supports probabilistic thinking and evidence-based decision making.
- **Cognitive Dissonance Mechanisms:** Allows resolution and learning from conflicting beliefs.
- **Behavioral Economics:** Accounts for bias and rationality in decision processes.

---

## Architecture Highlights

- **Punishes incoherence and rigid dogma**
- **Encourages adaptive reasoning in novel situations**
- **Supports identity continuity across contexts**
- **Growth through structured internal pressure (self-evaluation)**
- **Mechanistically grounded cognitive dissonance resolution**
